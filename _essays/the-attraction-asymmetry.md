## Introduction

Attraction operates differently for men and women, not because of cultural conditioning alone, but because of deep evolutionary asymmetries in reproductive strategy. Understanding these differences is essential for navigating modern dating honestly.

## Main Argument

The core asymmetry is one of risk. Women face higher biological risk from a poor mate choice, which drives selectivity. Men face lower per-interaction risk, which drives volume. These baseline strategies shape everything from first impressions to long-term relationship dynamics.

This is not deterministic. Culture, individual personality, and context all modulate these tendencies. But ignoring the baseline means misunderstanding the system you are operating within.

The practical implication is that effective dating strategy looks different depending on which side of the asymmetry you sit on. Advice that ignores this difference is incomplete at best.

## Implications

Men benefit from demonstrating reliability and investment capacity. Women benefit from efficient filtering and honest assessment of compatibility signals. Both benefit from understanding that the other side is operating under different constraints.

## Conclusion

Attraction asymmetry is not a problem to solve. It is a feature of the system to understand. The couples who navigate it successfully are those who recognise and respect the different pressures each partner faces.
