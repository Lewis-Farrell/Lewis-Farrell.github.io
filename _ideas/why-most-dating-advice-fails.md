Most dating advice fails because it optimises for the wrong variable. It tells you how to get more dates, more matches, or more attention. But more is not the goal. Better is.

The person who goes on fifty mediocre dates has not outperformed the person who goes on three excellent ones. Volume-based strategies generate noise. Quality-based strategies generate signal.

The best dating advice is structural: understand your own patterns, clarify what you actually value, and build a system for finding people who share those values. Everything else is tactics applied to the wrong problem.
